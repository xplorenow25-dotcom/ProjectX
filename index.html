Crypto Education Hub

Master the fundamentals before utilizing the calculators.

Basics

1. Introduction to Trading

Trading cryptocurrencies involves speculating on price movements. Before risking capital, you must understand market cycles, bid/ask spreads, and order book dynamics.

 Trading

2. Spot vs. Futures

Spot trading means directly purchasing the asset. Futures trading involves contracts predicting future price action, often enhanced with leverage which increases both risk and reward.

 Risk

3. Risk Management

Never risk more than 1-2% of your portfolio on a single trade. Implementing proper stop losses and calculating position sizes is the foundation of longevity in the market.

 Trading

4. Understanding Leverage

Leverage amplifies your buying power by borrowing funds from the exchange. While 10x leverage magnifies profits tenfold, a mere 10% move against you leads to total liquidation.

 Analysis

5. Market vs. Limit Orders

Market orders execute immediately at the best available price, incurring higher "taker" fees. Limit orders sit on the order book until price reaches your target, utilizing lower "maker" fees.

 Psychology

6. Trading Psychology

Emotions like FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) and panic selling destroy portfolios. A mechanical approach relying on mathematical risk-to-reward ratios protects you from emotional decisions.

 Analysis

7. Technical vs. Fundamental

Technical analysis focuses on chart patterns and volume, while fundamental analysis evaluates the underlying technology, team, and tokenomics of a cryptocurrency project.

 Finance

8. Exchange Fees Explained

Exchanges make money on volume. Your profitability relies on minimizing taker fees, avoiding excessive trading (overtrading), and considering withdrawal network fees.

 Security

9. Protecting Your Capital

Not your keys, not your coins. Always store long-term holdings in cold hardware wallets. Only keep trading capital on active, reputable centralized or decentralized exchanges.
